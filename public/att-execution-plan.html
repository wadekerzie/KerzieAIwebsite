The AI Execution Plan

Thirty days. Eighteen team members. A defined, verified operating level for every one of them.

The end state in one sentence: every rep walks into every meeting already prepped, already aligned, and the team's account picture lives in one place.

The Objective

In 30 days, move all 18 members of the AT&T account team from wherever they are today to a defined, verified operating level with Claude, so the team runs on a shared account execution rhythm instead of 18 individual experiments.

This is not AI training. Nobody watches videos. Every milestone below is something a team member does, with their hands on the keyboard, verified in their own account.

How It Works: Assess, Group, Level Up

Week one starts with a working session with team leadership. We define the outcomes this team must be able to produce, then build a custom AI Execution Assessment backwards from those outcomes, specific to WWT and the AT&T account. All 18 members take it that same week.

The assessment does two jobs. It gives leadership an honest baseline, and it lets us group people by where they actually are. Cohorts are formed by proficiency level, so nobody sits through material below their level and nobody gets left at the starting line. Sessions run virtually in small groups, with in-person available where it accelerates things.

Progress is tracked against three levels. Leadership sees exactly who has reached what level at every point in the engagement.

The Three Levels

Level 1: Established

Every team member, by end of week 2
  • Custom instructions populated: WWT principles digitized, their role, and AT&T account team context. That field is never empty again.
  • A master context brain: a working project built from their own 30-plus minute voice memo covering responsibilities, reporting rhythms, systems, and relationships, expanded through a structured follow-up interview with Claude.
  • Basic fluency check passed: navigates projects, retrieves prior work, and continues a working thread without help.

Level 2: Operating

Every team member, by day 30 · verified on real work, not exercises
  • Ran a complete meeting cycle: pre-meeting brief from their context brain, meeting captured, transcript fed back, follow-ups and action items extracted.
  • Produced a management-ready weekly update from working context in minutes, not hours.
  • Generated Salesforce field updates from meeting output instead of typing them from memory.
  • Produced a pipeline update from their working context: opportunity movement, stalled-deal flags, and forecast changes within their slice of the AT&T account, ready to roll up.
  • Established the recording habit: business conversations flow into their context brain as source material.
  • Passed the prep-ready test: for a scheduled customer meeting, ready the day it was scheduled, not the night before.

Level 3: Compounding

Quick adopters, as many as get there
  • Built at least one recurring intelligence routine: competitor and market monitoring, executive sound-bite tracking, or account news feeding briefs automatically.
  • Contributing to the shared team dashboard.
  • Taught one workflow back to the team. This is how the team keeps leveling after the engagement ends.

Realistic expectation: everyone reaches Level 2, and an engagement like this typically produces 2 or 3 rock stars at Level 3 who become the team's internal engine.

Team-Level Milestones

  • WWT principles digitized as a shared custom instruction block every member runs.
  • A single-URL shared AT&T account dashboard, version 1, hosted inside WWT's own environment: projects, initiatives, responsibilities, pipeline picture, and account intelligence in one place. Nothing about this account ever lives outside WWT's walls.
  • A team pipeline rollup: every member's opportunity movement and forecast changes feeding one aggregate view, so leadership sees the account's pipeline narrative without chasing eighteen spreadsheets.
  • Leadership scoreboard: baseline and closing assessment results for all 18, level attainment, and usage trend. WWT already tracks usage, so the adoption curve shows up in numbers leadership already looks at.

The 30-Day Schedule

Week 1
Alignment and baseline. Leadership outcomes session, custom assessment built and taken by all 18, cohorts formed by proficiency level, voice memo homework assigned.
Week 2
Foundation. Cohort working sessions plus short 1:1 slots. Every member leaves week 2 at Level 1.
Week 3
The meeting cycle and the reporting rhythm, run on live meetings from each person's actual calendar: briefs, debriefs, weekly updates, Salesforce extraction, and pipeline rollups. Quick adopters identified and given Level 3 tracks.
Week 4
Compounding and verification. Team dashboard assembled, Level 3 pushes, closing assessment against the milestone checklist, and a leadership readout: scoreboard, before and after, and the operating rhythm going forward.
